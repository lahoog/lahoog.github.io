Work from the Upper Mississippi River Basin Association

In May 2021, I joined the Upper Mississippi River Basin Association as a GIS Intern. The project I worked on was to determine the potential ecosystem beneifts from the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers (USACE) doing water level drawdowns in the Upper Mississippi River. The project used the Dabbling Duck Migration Model, developed by the USACE to "evaluate the general quality of fall migration habitat on the Upper Mississippi River for a variety of dabbling duck species." To evaluate the quality of habitat, a Habitat Suitability Index (HSI) was calculated using 11 different parameters:

  • distance to bottomland hardwoods
  • Distance to cropland and cropland practices
  • Water depth 4-18 inches in the fall
  • Water depth less than 4 inches in the fall
  • Percent open water (variable 5)
  • Plant community diversity (variable 6)
  • Important food plant coverage (variable 7)
  • Percent of area containing loafing structures
  • Structure to provide thermal protection
  • Disturbance in the fall
  • Visual barriers

Water level drawdowns affect variables 5, 6, and 7 from above and 4 different drawdown depths were evaluated to determine which depth(s) produced the optimal HSI for each river pool: 0.5', 1', 1.5', and 2'. Datasets such as water surface shapefiles, land cover/use shapefiles, and vegetation sample points were used in the GIS analysis. Model scores were gleaned from the GIS analysis results, showing which river pools have the most suitable habitat at all 4 drawdown depths compared to their HSI for normal drawdown conditions.
